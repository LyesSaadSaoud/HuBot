Abstract

HuBot is a biomimetic mobile robot designed to study and conserve endangered Houbara bustards. Equipped with YOLOv9 for detection and real-time deep learning, HuBot enables precise tracking of bird behaviors, social interactions, and habitat use. Tested in extreme conditions, HuBot represents a significant advancement in non-invasive ecological monitoring.

Experimental Setup

HuBot was evaluated on NVIDIA Jetson AGX Xavier with PyTorch 2.2.1, CUDA 11.8, and OpenCV for real-time object detection and data analysis.

Dataset: 5,000 images of Houbara bustards annotated with bounding boxes. The dataset is split into training (4,000), validation (500), and testing (500).

Environmental Testing: Conducted in Abu Dhabi, UAE, under extreme temperature and humidity conditions.
